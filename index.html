Eleocharis acicularis (Dwarf Hairgrass) is a popular aquarium foreground plant often used in aquascapes to create a grass-like carpet look. Carpet Plants

Carpeting plants such as Hemianthus callitrichoides and Glossostigma elatinoides and grasses such as Eleocharis parvula are very economical plants to work with. This is because they can be separated into quite small portions and spaced apart to achieve an eventual mat or lawn effect, known as a ‘carpet’. It is possible to get around 20 or more plantlets out of some good quality pots, depending of course on how much effort the planter is prepared to put in.

Stem Plants

Stem plants are popular, often fast-growing aquatic plants for the background or middle ground of an aquarium or aquascape. They are traditionally planted into the substrate. Under good nutrient conditions and sufficient light, these plants soon develop a new root system, and stems and foliage can reach the entire height of an aquarium tank in a short time. Due to the variety of species you can fall back on many different leaf shapes and colors for the aquarium design. In addition to the familiar shades of green especially reddish stem plants create a particularly beautiful color accentuation. These species are usually a bit more demanding in keeping.
